Bay Area Restaurant Recommendations

Compiled from my FoodTalkCentral reviews (2022-2026) and other personal notes, with the help of Claude Opus 4.5.

Fine Dining ($$$$)

Restaurant Location Cuisine Notes FTC Review Excitement
Commis Oakland Modern American Tasting My favorite 2-star place I've tried so far in the Bay, in the last few years. Highlights: onion financier ("mad genius level"), beetroot prep, slow poached egg with smoked dates. Link 4
The Shota SF Omakase Somewhat better than the two proper Omakase spots in the East Bay, but is $300/head (and in SF). Would be in my top-10 but not top-3 in LA. - 4
Lazy Bear SF Modern American Tasting Interesting, but am sort of mixed on whether it felt "worth it". Am glad I went once, though. Course note cards provided. Highlights: Dungeness crab (10/10), dandelion cacao pudding. Mixed: eggs (not enough bacon), kohlrabi. Link 3
Californios SF Mexican Tasting Was a lot of corn, though the menu does change a lot. Highlights: soup course, final fish course. Mixed: early courses "just good", desserts mixed, pacing too slow (3.5 hrs). Other people on FTC are huge fans, so idk. Link 3
Nisei SF Modern Kaiseki Very pretty, mostly quite tasty, but nothing was a "knockout." Not particularly memorable. Link 2

High-End ($$$)

Sushi/Japanese

Restaurant Location Cuisine Notes FTC Review Excitement
Utzutzu Alameda Omakase ~75% of top LA places at half the price. Hidden entrance. Highlights: hirame, shima aji, maguro, saba, tako no sumiso, temaki. Mixed: A5 wagyu "not the star." Link 3.5
Sushi Salon Oakland Omakase Exceptional neta quality, good rice. Can reorder favorites. Portions slightly small. Link 3.5
Delage Oakland Japanese Japanese w/sushi courses in between. $100 = "a steal." Highlights: bamboo-wrapped sushi, gindara katsu, akami & isaki nigiri, pomegranate mochi. Mixed: katsuo tataki "too busy." Link 2.5
Tancho Castro Valley Omakase $120(?) baseline is a steal; supplements overpriced. Highlights: engawa ("winner of the night"), jasmine tea jello. Mixed: snow crab "ok." Link 2.5

San Ho Won SF (Mission) Korean BBQ Default/free banchan (all of them), galbi mandu, gyeran-jjim (egg souffle), grilled tteok, double-cut galbi, neck tongue, ssamjang & house chili oil, bingsu On-menu banchan Nothing like other kbbq places; you don't cook anything yourself and the meat is much higher quality. Don't bother with the tasting menu and just order a la carte. Link 5

Burdell Oakland Southern Marinated cauliflower ("best ever"), lamb ribs ("astonishingly tender"), albacore tartare, cathead biscuits - Strong across the menu, pretty "classic" upscale restaurant style (menu, decorations, patrons). Link 5

Mid-Range ($$)

Restaurant Location Cuisine Exceptional Items Skip/Mixed Notes FTC Review Excitement
Mujiri Oakland Sushi All fish, rice quality Slight oversaucing on some pieces The one good mid-tier sushi place in the East Bay. Primarily takeout with bar seats. Link 3
Sfizio Oakland Italian Menu changes frequently. Grilled vegetables & desserts are usually great. Focaccia ("weird") Hybrid service. Could charge double for pasta/dessert quality. Link 4
Soba Ichi Oakland Soba Soba noodles, soba-cha gelato - Definitely get the gelato. - 3
Kaokao Grill Berkeley Asian BBQ Fusion - - Weird Asian BBQ fusion, very surprisingly good. Tiny menu. Generally order delivery. - 2
Kiraku Berkeley Izakaya Grilled smoked unagi ("Bite of the night!"), pork jowl, beef intestine - Can't make reservations online. Very cramped inside. Worth it. Link 5
Great China Berkeley Chinese Tea-smoked duck, "Ant Climbed the Tree", dumplings, garlic fried rice Salt & pepper prawns (not crispy), sauteed crab (flavor "just not there") - Link 2
Moobongri Oakland Korean Soup Rice cake & dumpling soup - Generally order delivery; have only been in person once. Not a "destination" but fun with friends. - 2

Casual/Budget ($)

Restaurant Location Cuisine Exceptional Items Skip/Mixed Notes FTC Review Excitement
Tsuruya Berkeley Ramen Chicken chashu ("best I've had"), bamboo shoots, broth, jammy egg - Best ramen in Berkeley. Link 4
Tahina Alameda Mediterranean Eggplant dip, pita. - The one decent Mediterranean spot I've found up here. Never been in person, always ordered via DoorDash. - 3
Jungdon Katsu East Bay Katsu Katsu - Used to be a ghost kitchen, ordered via DoorDash/etc. Apparently now has an Emeryville location (haven't been). "Better katsu than any specialist, including Kagura." Link 3
Huangcheng Noodle House Oakland Chinese Noodles Chongqing street noodles with knife-cut noodles - Good both as soup & "dry" (with sauce). Better in person than delivery. - 2
Poppy Bagels East Bay Bagels Bagels (especially as sandwiches) - Good texture, acceptable flavor. Reportedly work well with toppings. Link 2
The Smokehouse Berkeley Burgers "Correct" thin hamburgers, much less greasy Fries (terrible) Unlike everyone else doing that style of fast food hamburger. - 2
North Berkeley BART Taco Stand Berkeley Tacos Tripe - Open till midnight (2am Fri/Sat). Self-service toppings. An extra point for being open late and it's a vibe to eat tacos outdoors with friends. Link 3
